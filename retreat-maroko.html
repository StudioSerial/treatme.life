O retreatuAbout the retreat

Što je ovo, zapravo. What this actually is.

Maroko nije destinacija za odmaranje, ali Taghazout jeste. Atlantski valovi, ribarski brodovi u zaljevu, Salemova majka koja donosi svježe povrće s tržnice. Popodneva koja mirišu na kumin. Yoga na terasi s pogledom na ocean. Tišina koja nije praznina, nego punina. Morocco isn't a destination for resting, but Taghazout is. The Atlantic wave, fishing boats in the bay, Salem's mother bringing fresh vegetables from the market. Afternoons that smell of cumin. Yoga on a terrace overlooking the ocean. Silence that isn't emptiness, but fullness.

Salem kuha svakodnevno iz obiteljskih recepata - tagine, khubz, začinjena kaša, svježe povrće. Petra vodi jutarnju yogu s pogledom na Atlantik, večernja yin je nešto posebno u toj tišini. Ines drži prostor da sve teče nevidljivo. Salem cooks daily from family recipes, tagine, khubz, spiced porridge, fresh vegetables. Petra leads morning yoga with Atlantic views, evening yin is something special in that silence. Ines holds the space so everything flows invisibly.

Maksimalno 10 gostiju. Sedam dana koji imaju drugačiji ritam od svega što poznaješ. Maroko koji upoznaješ kroz ljude koji ga zovu domom, ne kroz turistički itinerar. Maximum 10 guests. Seven days with a rhythm unlike anything you know. Morocco experienced through the people who call it home, not through a tourist itinerary.

Taghazout.
Salemov grad. Taghazout.
Salem's town.

Taghazout je surfersko-ribarsko mjesto 20 km sjeverno od Agadira, uz Atlantski ocean. Bijele kuće na stijenama, tirkizno more, miris soli i kumina. U listopadu, mirno, toplo, savršeno za usporavanje. To nije turistički resort, to je pravi grad, s pravim ljudima. I to je upravo smisao. Taghazout is a surf-fishing village 20 km north of Agadir, on the Atlantic ocean. White houses on the cliffs, turquoise sea, the scent of salt and cumin. In October, quiet, warm, perfect for slowing down. This isn't a tourist resort, it's a real town, with real people. And that's exactly the point.

FAQ

Pitanja o Maroku. Questions about Morocco.

Trebam li vizu za Maroko? Do I need a visa for Morocco?
Za građane EU (i Hrvatska), ne. Maroko ne zahtijeva vizu za kraće boravke do 90 dana. Putovnica je obavezna (osobna karta nije dovoljna). Javi nam ako si iz druge zemlje i možemo provjeriti za tebe. For EU citizens (including Croatia), no. Morocco doesn't require a visa for stays under 90 days. A passport is required (ID card alone isn't sufficient). Let us know if you're from another country and we can check for you.
Kakvo je more u listopadu? What's the sea like in October?
Atlantski ocean u Taghazoutu je u listopadu ~22°C, savršeno za plivanje. Dani su topli (25—28°C), noći blage. Nema turista, nema gužve. To je jedan od najljepših trenutaka za posjetiti Maroko. The Atlantic in Taghazout in October is ~22°C, perfect for swimming. Days are warm (25—28°C), nights mild. No tourists, no crowds. It's one of the most beautiful times to visit Morocco.
Je li hrana zaista biljnog porijekla? Is the food really plant-based?
Da, u potpunosti. Ali marokanska biljna kuhinja nije prazna, punjena je začinima, orašastim plodovima, maslinovim uljem, svježim povrćem i aromama koje nemaš doma. Salem kuha iz obiteljskih recepata i svaki obrok je priča. Ako imaš alergija, javi nam pri prijavi. Yes, fully. But Moroccan plant-based cooking isn't sparse, it's filled with spices, nuts, olive oil, fresh vegetables and aromas you don't have at home. Salem cooks from family recipes and every meal is a story. If you have allergies, let us know when you register.
